Galápagos Sea Lion

Zalophus wollebaeki
IUCN Red List Status: ENDANGERED (EN)

Habitat & Range

This species is endemic strictly to the Galápagos Islands (Ecuador), with a tiny breeding colony established on Isla de la Plata just off the coast of mainland Ecuador. They prefer sandy shores and rocky shorelines where they can easily access the ocean to hunt.

Primary Threats

The greatest threat to the Galápagos Sea Lion is climate change, specifically the increasing frequency and severity of El Niño events. During an El Niño, the ocean currents change, reducing the nutrient-rich upwellings. This causes a massive collapse in their primary food sources (sardines and other small fish), leading to widespread starvation, especially among pups and juveniles.

Population Estimates

Current estimates suggest there are fewer than 10,000 mature individuals remaining in the wild. The population experiences severe fluctuations based on climate conditions, making recovery highly volatile.
